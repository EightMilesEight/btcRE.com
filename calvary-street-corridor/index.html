Calvary Street Corridor

The Calvary Street Corridor is ready for its next act.

Nestled on the banks of the Charles River Greenway in Waltham, Massachusetts, Calvary Street hosts a remarkable stretch of late 19th and early 20th century mills, factories and warehouses that were – until recently – often underutilized.

Today, thanks to a renewed appreciation for Waltham’s many charms and ideal location within the greater Boston metro, as well as the natural resources of the Charles River and creative reuse of once-obsolete buildings, the Calvary Street Corridor is undergoing a remarkable transformation.

Accelerated by local real estate development firm btcRE, the Corridor has become a burgeoning hub of cutting-edge businesses and green and clean technologies.

The focus on innovative and eco-friendly technologies ensures Calvary Street Corridor revitalization efforts remain sustainable. The restored buildings are energy-efficient, and the green tech spaces promote practices that benefit the economy and the environment.

The Charles River Greenway, with its lush landscapes and walking and biking trail, provide a perfect backdrop for this new chapter in Waltham's history.

There are many incredible amenities just a short distance away – ranging from gazpacho to groceries, coffee to custom cocktails.

And even a little yoga, if that’s your thing.
